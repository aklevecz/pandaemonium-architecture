“Things have found a way of avoiding a dialectic of meaning that was beginning to bore them." – Jean Baudrillard, *Fatal Strategies*

On the road to the realization of Lyotard’s “pervasive global automaton”, Weeping Angel plays a small but singular role, a dialectical reversal of the consumer model a

its paradigm of personal control, asking: *what if we can’t turn it off?* The operation weaponized that icon of American and English culture—the television—automating means of a software injection, simple deceptions—turning off the screen and switching off the “power” LED—so that the infected TV appeared to be off while collecting and transmitting network and audio data., turning the near-ubiquitous “reciever” into a “transmitter”, a listening device. The two-way television in Orwell’s *1984*, collecting audio and video while broadcasting and essentially never “off”, was an obvious model. Another, less obvious model is linked by way of the operation’s cryptonym to the long-running British scifi show Doctor Who: Weeping Angels, alien entities that disguise themselves as statues when observed, first appeared on the show around the time of the Weeping Angel covert operation. From a Dr. Who fan site:

> WEEPING ANGELS appear as winged, humanoid statues, often with a weeping or mournful expression.
>
> Quantum-Locked: They can only move when not being observed, meaning they appear as statues when someone looks at them. [^2]

A collaborative UK MI5/US BTSS (Bioengineering, Technology, and Surgical Sciences within the US National Institutes of Health (NIH)), WEEPING ANGEL equipped Samsung F Series SmartTVs with a “Fake Off” mode. The program illegally targeted domestic populations in the U.S. and U.K. but also, importantly, instantiated an imaginary that reversed the command/control hierarchy for consumer devices, much like the subject/object switch posited by Jean Baudrillard in *Fatal Strategies*:

> “Things have found a way of avoiding a dialectic of meaning that was beginning to bore them.” [^3]

The infected tv, when shut off, appears like any other consumer appliance in “off” mode: dead screen, no green LED. The relationship between the Weeping Angel alien entity, the software implant, and the surveillance program’s seemingly multiple goals is of more than metanymic importance: the “quantum locked” aliens’ static, stone-like

immobility while “awake” mirrors the dead screen’s concealment of the “one-way (black) mirror” of automated surveillance. This is just one of the ways in which Lyotard’s *cognitive automaton* “plays dead”, assuming the appearance of a “dumb (or ‘dead’) object” rather than an agent of active data collection. The tendency of the automaton to veil itself throws into question our relationship with all of the “dumb (‘dead’) objects” that surround us. Further, the “magical” quality of the Weeping Angel entities conveys itself to the intelligence gathering operation, adding a mystical veneer to state-sanctioned illegality– to the electronic stalking and violation of the rights of a public caught unawares and in thrall to consumerism, its codes, and its symbolic order.

From the Oxford Languages online dictionary:

**in·duce·ment** [in'dōosmənt]

noun

a thing that persuades or influences someone to do something: "*companies were prepared to build only in return for massive inducements*" · "*there is no inducement to work for payment*" [^4]

The inducements of new technologies extend far beyond their ability to deliver content or mere use-value to include this fantastical, “magical” quality, something like a seduction performed by a hypnotist. Philosophers have, for some time, suggested that the AI assemblage / Lyotard’s cognitive automaton is essentially co-extensive with capitalism. As Todd McGowan puts it in *Capitalism and Desire*:

“The resilience of capitalism as an economic or social form derives from its relationship to the psyche and to how subjects relate to their own satisfaction.” [^5]

The myriad inducements offered by new technologies proliferate in the same way as those offered by capitalism, providing psychic satisfaction and the (partial) fulfillment of unmet desires.
