page is no longer the digest itself; it's the index plus a few
cross-cutting themes the TA surfaced while reading everything.

## How to read this

- Need a specific reading? Go to `readings/intro/` or
  `readings/week-NN/` and open the digest.
- Need an overview of what a week is doing? Read the three-to-six
  digests in that week's folder.
- Don't know where to start? See the **recommended reading order for
  the TA** below.

## Cross-cutting themes

Patterns that emerged from reading everything that the four stances
don't quite capture.

### 1. Adjacent vocabularies for reversal

Stance 4 (reversal / opacity) has *many* adjacent terms across the
readings. A student can pick the one that fits their project:

- **Fog / zones of offensive opacity** — Tiqqun
- **Obfuscation / chaff / TrackMeNot / Spartacus** — Brunton &
  Nissenbaum (practical taxonomy)
- **Ritual of reversal** — Vogt (anthropological)
- **Seeking the outside** — Colquhoun (via Fisher)
- **Subtraction** — Roden (posthumanist)
- **Shattering the globe** — Colebrook (ecological)
- **Counter-algorithmic work** — Doctorow (labor)
- **Vaporware / RumINT / art-as-rumor** — Benzel *Polybius*

This is not a single stance with one name. It's a family. Any of these
terms can anchor a project. The lab page should present the family,
not pick one.

### 2. What the apparatus "decided" — historical dimension

McQuillan, Barad, and Bratton all make the same move at different
scales: the apparatus has already enacted cuts before the user
arrives. But several readings add a *historical* dimension I want to
name:

- **Selfridge** (1958) is the original demon architecture — the
  cuts are 67 years old.
- **Minsky & Papert** (*Perceptrons*, 1969) almost killed neural net
  research; AI was stuck on their critique for a decade. The apparatus
  also decides *when* something is thinkable.
- **Good** (1965, *Ultraintelligent Machine*) seeds the
  intelligence-explosion narrative that now shapes TESCREAL.
- **Goodfellow** (2014, GANs) — the adversarial frame came from a
  specific technical moment and now structures all generative
  aesthetics.

Useful for the lab: when you open Stable Diffusion, you're touching
decisions made in 1958, 1965, 1969, 2014, and last Tuesday. Make that
historical layering visible in critique.

### 3. Two responses to surveillance: redact or disappear

Week 2 is the cleanest case. Andreessen's *Techno-Optimist Manifesto*
is the apparatus. Grosser's redaction and Bazzell's *Extreme Privacy*
are two opposed but equally lab-relevant responses:

- **Grosser**: stay in the document, delete almost everything, let
  the residue speak. A *reversal* move.
- **Bazzell**: leave the document entirely; become unfindable; build
  a zone of opacity. A *disappearance* move.

These are two valid project modes. A student doesn't have to pick;
they can sit between.

### 4. Obfuscation-as-defense vs. fogging-as-attack

Week 11 pairs Brunton & Nissenbaum (obfuscation as tool for the
*weak*) with the GNET Report (fogging/flooding as extremist propaganda
tactic). Same tactic, opposite politics. Important pedagogical
tension. Students proposing an opacity move should be asked: *whose
side is your fog on?* Not every obfuscation is progressive.

### 5. Gigantic or ignored: the technical-paper problem

Several assigned additional readings are actual CS/ML papers: Vaswani
(attention), Goodfellow (GAN), Brock (BigGAN), Rombach (latent
diffusion), Mercer (speech recognition). For art students these are
hard; their value is archaeological (what assumptions the architecture
bakes in), not technical. Lab should teach *reading* these papers for
cuts and priors, not for reimplementation. They're apparatuses to
interrogate, not instructions to follow.

### 6. The "cute cryptonym" aesthetic — Benzel's *Foxacid* move

Benzel's Week 4 note pulls out something I'd missed: state
surveillance operations pick deliberately cute names (Foxacid,
Quantumsquirrel, Weeping Angel) as a form of *affective management*.
This is both a thing to critique and a tactic the lab can deploy —
cute-named reversal projects that hide their politics in their
adorability. A possible project type.
