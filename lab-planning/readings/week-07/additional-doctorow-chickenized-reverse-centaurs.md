## Summary

Doctorow examines "chickenization" — a labor exploitation model where firms control every aspect of operation while workers absorb risk — as it metastasizes in the gig economy via algorithms. Contrasts "centaurs" (humans augmented by beneficial AI) with "reverse-centaurs" (humans puppeteered by AI), showing how apps like DoorDash and Amazon transform workers into bodies executing algorithmic heads. Celebrates counter-algorithmic resistance: tree-hanging phone spoofing, Indonesia's *tuyul* apps that modify dispatch algorithms in workers' favor. Worker power requires seizing the means of computation.

## Key quotes

- *"The AI is in charge, and the human is the junior partner. The AI is the head, telling the body what to do. The driver is the body — the slow-witted, ambulatory meat that is puppeteered by the AI master."* (line 57)
- *"Counter-algorithmic activism is subtly different from Para's model: While Para exposes hidden information and automates the process of uncovering it, counter-algorithmic work actually seeks to turn workers into centaurs, not reverse-centaurs."* (line 103)

## Why it's in the course

Week 7's seminar topics explicitly name *"Bossware v. Chickenized Reverse Centaurs"* — with Para, Tuyul, et al. Doctorow supplies the phrase and its working politics. Directly materializes the week's critique of algorithmic management.

## Relevance to the lab

**Apparatus** and **Reversal.** Names what the algorithm "decided" (your route, your payout, your bodily movements). The Reversal stance is active here: students reverse algorithmic control not by accepting or optimizing within it, but by modifying the apparatus itself (tuyul apps, counter-algorithmic tooling). The worker–algorithm feedback loop becomes artistic material when inverted.
