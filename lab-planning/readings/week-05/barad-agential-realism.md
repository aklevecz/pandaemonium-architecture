## Summary

Barad reconceives agency, causality, and objectivity without relying on human intentionality or the subject/object divide. Drawing on quantum physics and Niels Bohr's complementarity principle, she argues that phenomena emerge through *intra-actions* — material-discursive entanglements where the apparatus itself is causally significant. Neither observers nor observed are pre-given; both are produced through the specific configurations of measurement/apparatus. Objectivity is not secured by spatial separation but by "agential separability" — the enacted cut within phenomena. Rejects humanist agency while preserving responsibility and ethics as built into the material dynamics of the world's becoming.

## Key quotes

- *"Apparatuses are not passive observing instruments; on the contrary, they are productive of (and part of) phenomena."* (p. 146)
- *"Apparatuses are specific material-discursive practices (they are not merely laboratory setups that embody human concepts and take measurements); apparatuses produce differences that matter — they are boundary-making practices that are formative of matter and meaning."* (p. 148)
- *"Relations do not follow relata, but the other way around."* (p. 136–137)
- *"Agency is not an attribute but the ongoing reconfigurings of the world."* (p. 141)
- *"Cuts are agentially enacted not by willful individuals but by the larger material arrangement of which 'we' are a 'part.' The cuts that we participate in enacting matter."* (lines 229–230)

## Why it's in the course

Barad is foundational for **stance 1 (Apparatus)** alongside McQuillan. Her account of how apparatuses produce phenomena (not merely measure pre-existing reality) becomes the philosophical ground for understanding that AI systems are not passive calculators but active shapers of what comes to matter. The course uses Barad to insist that students name what the apparatus has already decided before they arrived. Week 5 pairs her with Bonabeau (swarm) and Benzel's Polybius note.

## Relevance to the lab

**Apparatus.** Theoretical core of this stance. Every design choice — trained model, dataset, loss function, interface — is an agential cut that materializes some phenomena while foreclosing others. The lab frame demands that students become accountable for these cuts. This is the reading students should encounter directly if they read any theory at all this semester.
