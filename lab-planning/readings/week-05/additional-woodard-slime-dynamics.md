## Summary

Woodard's short Zero Books monograph argues for a "dark vitalism" grounded in Schelling and the Speculative Realism of Grant, Brassier, Harman, and Meillassoux. He refuses both the anthropocentric progressivism of mainstream evolutionary narrative and the cozy Deleuzo-Guattarian vitalism, proposing instead a spatio-temporal vitalism where "the vital force is time and its effect on space." The reading stays close to slime as the actual ontological condition — slime molds solving mazes without cognition, emergence versus reductionism, swarms versus individuals — and reaches Lovecraft and Warhammer 40K's Tyranids as the culmination of "the teeming biological… a creeping abyss of biology, where reason is only one feature amidst a taloned and toothed pandemonium." The last word is *pandemonium*; the seminar's title reappears as the frame of the text.

## Key quotes

- *"The vaunted progress of life is really random motion away from simple beginnings, not directed impetus toward inherently advantageous complexity."* (line 31, Woodard quoting Stephen Jay Gould)
- *"For humans, the mindless functioning of life, of organisms moving towards goals without any form of intelligence, of creatures that function in a completely bottom up fashion reasserts not only the accidence of thought but also thought's unimportance for survival."* (line 37)
- *"The vitalism we will be pursuing here avoids this connect in that it is not a theory that asserts a vital substance or stuff that propels life forward, but that the vital force is time and its effect on space."* (line 45)
- *"Intelligent life is an environmental cancer."* (line 155, Woodard citing Warren Fahy's character Redmond)
- *"The teeming biological, if beginning from a unity and moving outwards, dividing into ever more chaotic and divergent forms creates a creeping abyss of biology, where reason is only one feature amidst a taloned and toothed pandemonium."* (line 177)

## Why it's in the course

Week 5's *Young Slime Life* is built directly on Woodard: "Swarm v. Slime Dynamics: Agent v. Swarm v. Protean Ooze… Roberta Breitmore, Lil Miquela, and the Brud; *The Brood*, OOOz, Slime Dynamics." Pairing Woodard with Bonabeau (swarm intelligence), Barad (agential realism), and Minsky/Papert (perceptrons) produces the week's core tension: bottom-up protean emergence against top-down linearly-separable classification. The text's closing word ("pandemonium") is a direct callback to the seminar's own metaphor — a cogent reason to include it here rather than simply in a philosophy course.
