# Adam Elkus, *Speedrunning Through The Language-Game*

**Week:** 3 (Primary)
**File:** `markdown/Adam Elkus, Speedrunning Through The Language-Game.md`
**Length:** 50 lines

## Summary

Essay on the collapse of "believing in words" in online discourse. Opens
with Sartre's description of anti-Semites as playing ironic games ("the
anti-Semites have the right to play") and argues that this model is no
longer sufficient to describe contemporary trolling, irony, and "language-
game" behavior. Elkus's diagnosis: the emerging dominant personality is
one "unbound by consistency," because "maintaining the fiction of a
coherent unified self is just too hard in an era when everything is just
streams of data." Closes with references to *Ghost in the Shell*, *Serial
Experiments Lain*, and the 1730 Great Cat Massacre — identities as
retroactive reconstructions from fragmentary data.

## Key quotes

- Sartre (quoted): *"The anti-Semites have the right to play… They delight
  in acting in bad faith, since they seek not to persuade by sound
  argument but to intimidate and disconcert. If you press them too
  closely, they will abruptly fall silent."* (line 9)
- Elkus on Arendt on Weimar: *"Mainstream figures made pious public
  proclamations of values they privately held in contempt. Subcultures
  and fringes valorized vulgarity and gangsterism."* (line 31)
- *"Perhaps the personality most optimized for success in the emerging
  environment is really someone that is unbound by consistency of any
  sort, because maintaining the fiction of a coherent unified self is
  just too hard in an era when everything is just streams of data."*
  (line 47)
- On the "post-self" not being visibly posthuman: *"There will be no one
  moment — akin to the infamous transformation of 2001's Dr. Dave Bowman
  into a star child — in which it theatrically transcends its own
  humanity and leaves the rest of us mere mortals behind."* (line 51)
- Cited from *Ghost in the Shell*'s Project 2501: *"All things change in
  a dynamic environment. Your effort to remain what you are is what
  limits you."* (line 45)

## Why it's in the course

Week 3's seminar is *Degrade the Threads*: enshittification, Baudrillard's
evil demon of images, AI slopworld, consensus reality vs. burbclaves.
Elkus provides the diagnostic for *why* the language-games of 2019-plus
discourse no longer reward sincerity — a prerequisite for understanding
AI-slop's rhetorical and social dynamics. Pairs with Baudrillard (same
week) and Benzel's *New Memory* note.

## Relevance to the lab

**Medium.** Elkus is useful as a text about how art-making-with-sincerity
became structurally harder in the post-2015 information environment. A
student whose project engages online discourse (memes, forum posts, slop,
shitposting as medium) needs this frame — both to understand that
sincerity is not a neutral default and to resist the easy move of pure
irony. Not a core lab-frame text, but a hedge against a common mistake:
students producing ironic AI-art who haven't reckoned with what irony
costs.

## Caveat

Some dated references (2019 context, Trump-era discourse, *Tabletmag*
citation). Doesn't date the argument, but a student encountering it cold
may miss the urgency.
