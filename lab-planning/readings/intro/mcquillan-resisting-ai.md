## Summary

McQuillan demystifies AI's technical operations — machine learning, deep learning, neural networks, optimization — to expose its political implications. AI is not neutral; it is *apparatus*: technology + institutions + ideology operating as feedback loops. McQuillan traces how AI amplifies existing inequalities, enables austerity, and creates conditions for fascist solutions to social crisis. Rather than futurology about superintelligence, he examines what AI actually does: creates zones of exception, targets marginal populations, naturalizes structural violence. Resistance requires alternatives rooted in feminist epistemology, mutual aid, and worker self-organization — not regulation, which leaves the apparatus intact.

## Key quotes

- *"The apparatus of AI forms feedback loops with the rest of society: it's 'a structured structure that becomes a structuring structure' (Bourdieu, 1980)."* (line 17, also the course homepage epigraph)
- *"There's no actual intelligence in artificial intelligence. Machine learning is a set of mathematical operations of iteration and optimization."* (line 84)
- *"An anti-fascist approach is not simply one that opposes fascist tendencies but one that actively works towards structural alternatives to the conditions that give rise to the possibility of fascism in the first place."* (line 45)

## Why it's in the course

One of only two anchor texts (alongside Tiqqun). The homepage epigraph — *"The apparatus of AI forms feedback loops with the rest of society"* — is McQuillan's. He grounds the course materially: this is not abstract philosophy but analysis of actual harms (facial recognition failures, welfare algorithms, labor exploitation, data surveillance).

## Relevance to the lab

Load-bearing for **Apparatus**. McQuillan supplies the course's core definition of AI as apparatus and provides the political *why*: students need to understand that making art with/against AI is inseparable from resistance to structural inequality. Offers vocabulary (apparatus, feedback loops, zones of exception, opacity) that connects theory to praxis. The lab's emphasis on opacity aligns with his call for "zones of offensive opacity."
