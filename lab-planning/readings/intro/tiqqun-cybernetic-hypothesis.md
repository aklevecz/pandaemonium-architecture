## Summary

Cybernetics — the science of control, feedback, and self-regulation — has become the implicit operating system of late capitalism and the state. Rather than direct domination, the Empire works through feedback loops that pre-empt resistance by incorporating it. The cybernetic hypothesis colonizes not just labor but time, rhythm, and desire itself. Against this, Tiqqun proposes *zones of offensive opacity* (ZOO): places where information flows are interrupted, where bodies can experiment without being monitored. Resistance requires adopting other rhythms (slowness, pulsation, improvisation), creating "unrepresentable spaces," and refusing transparency and representation. Fog — haze, vapor, opacity — is the first condition of revolt.

## Why it's in the course

One of two anchor texts (alongside McQuillan). The course title page cites Deleuze's "Societies of Control" directly and Tiqqun provides the course's political spine — an anti-cybernetic program that runs underneath every week's seminar. Every discussion of platforms, captology, likewar, social sadism, and predictive analytics sits inside Tiqqun's frame.

## Relevance to the lab

Load-bearing for **Reversal / Zones of opacity**. The entire lab stance of "making work that refuses clarity and legibility" derives from this text. Key vocabulary the lab inherits: fog, haze, rhythm, improvisation, opacity, disconnection, ZOO. Critical caveat: Tiqqun teaches that resistance is *not representable in advance* — you cannot propose "how to use AI against itself" because any proposal becomes legible to the apparatus. Instead, the lab creates conditions: unrepresentable spaces, vacuoles of non-communication, rhythms that disrupt the binary pulse of control. The work is in opacity itself, not in what opacity might produce.
