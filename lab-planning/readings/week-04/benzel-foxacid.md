## Summary

Benzel examines three covert NSA/US intelligence operations named after fictional entities (the "cute" Fox, the Quantum Cat meme, Doctor Who's Weeping Angels), arguing the cryptonyms themselves are a form of *cutification* — softening the affective register of mass surveillance through aesthetic appeal. WEEPING ANGEL, a joint UK/US operation, weaponized Samsung Smart TVs, turning "off" into a *fake off* mode; the TV continues collecting audio while appearing dead. Benzel ties this to Lyotard's *Libidinal Economy* and its "pervasive global automaton" seeking to immobilize bodies. The Weeping Angel operation reverses the control hierarchy: the device appears inert (watched over by benevolent angels), but the spectator is actually *watched by* the machine. Contemporary inducements proliferate through the same mechanisms as capitalist desire; "animal spirit" cryptonyms are a form of *occultism in plain sight* — state magic disguised as cuteness.

## Why it's in the course

Week 4's seminar covers planetary computation, very slow AIs, pernicious feedback loops, Tiqqun vs. Lyotard, Capitalist Realism to Acid Communism. This note is Benzel's bridge between those theoretical poles: the cute-named operations are the material form of Lyotard's "pervasive global automaton" and the targets against which Tiqqun's zones of opacity would push.

## Relevance to the lab

Complicates all four stances. **Apparatus**: the apparatus is already covert, already operating before you consent. **Creative Act**: the feedback loop between artist and spectator is already being managed by state surveillance. **Loading**: the machine has been pre-loaded; your choice is whether to load against its native purpose. **Reversal**: Benzel suggests that recognizing the operation — naming Foxacid, Quantumsquirrel, Weeping Angel — is itself counter-practice, making visible what the state keeps classified. Students might make work that mimics the *form* of these operations: cute, seemingly innocent, but structured to reverse surveillance.
