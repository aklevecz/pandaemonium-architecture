# Matt Colquhoun, *Acid Communism*

**Week:** 4 (Primary)
**File:** `markdown/Matt Colquhoun, Acid Communism.md`
**Length:** 44 lines

## Summary

Short essay for *Krisis* (2018). Explains Mark Fisher's unfinished concept
"Acid Communism," arguing against Jeremy Gilbert's reduction of it to
1960s countercultural utopian recovery. Colquhoun insists Acid Communism
is a project for "seeking 'the outside' of sociopolitical hegemony," more
experimental than utopian, and grounded in Fisher's recurring note that
disturbing normality is itself disturbing: *"terrors are not all there is
to the outside"* (Fisher, *The Weird and the Eerie*). "Acid" is Deleuzian
desire — speculative, corrosive, denaturalising multiplicity — flowing
through communism "to create a-signifying feedback loops; an ideological
accelerator."

## Key quotes

- *"Acid Communism is a project beyond the pleasure principle. It is not
  only a project for the recuperation of the counterculture's lost
  potentials but also the expression of a desire for an experimental
  (rather than prescriptively utopian) leftist politics."* (line 11)
- Fisher, cited: *"The required subject — a collective subject — does
  not exist, yet the crisis, like all the other global crises we're now
  facing, demands that it be constructed."* (line 13)
- Deleuze, cited: *"There is never enough desire. Desire is the system
  of a-signifying signs out of which unconscious flows are produced in a
  social-historical field. … Desire is revolutionary because it is
  always seeking more connections."* (line 23)
- *"'Acid' is desire, as corrosive and denaturalising multiplicity,
  flowing through the multiplicities of communism itself to create
  a-signifying feedback loops; an ideological accelerator through which
  the new and previously unknown might be found in the politics we
  mistakenly think we already know."* (line 25)

## Why it's in the course

Week 4's seminar topic is *A Pervasive Global Cognitive Automaton*:
Bratton's planetary computation, Lyotard's *Libidinal Economy* vs.
Tiqqun's cybernetic hypothesis, and Fisher's arc from *Capitalist Realism*
to *Acid Communism*. Colquhoun gives the short primer on the latter,
pairing with Bratton (infrastructure) and Benzel's Foxacid note
(covert-ops aesthetics). The course's political horizon — what comes
*after* the cybernetic hypothesis — lives here.

## Relevance to the lab

**Medium-high.** Doesn't change the four-stance frame directly, but
supplies a useful adjacent vocabulary: "a-signifying feedback loops,"
"speculative desire," "seeking the outside." Students whose reversal move
wants a positive political horizon (not just refusal) can anchor it here.
Also pairs well with *New Memory* (Benzel W3) — both are texts about
assembling a different future rather than recovering a lost one.

## Caveats

Colquhoun depends heavily on Fisher and Deleuze. For students without
that background the piece will read as jargon. Worth pairing with the
Fisher *SF Capital* excerpt (W12) if used for lab.
