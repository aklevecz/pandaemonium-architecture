## Summary

Diederichsen dissects the double meaning of *Mehrwert* — colloquial "payoff/punch line" and Marxist "surplus value" — to diagnose a crisis in how contemporary art is legitimated, valued, and sold. He argues that contemporary art is caught in a pincer between a "culture of punch lines" (driven by advertising, curatorial patter, and a public trained to demand a single identifiable "claim" from every work) and a reactionary counter-coalition that fetishizes immersion and the ineffable. He then analyzes art's economy through Marxist categories, showing how the artist functions as both capitalist and self-exploited laborer, how speculation produces a doubly-false semblance of value, and how the "aura" has shifted from a physical index of the artist's touch to a "metaphysical index" of managerial/intellectual labor. The essay closes on a culture-industry crisis in which auratic-object production and a precarious performance proletariat recreate pre-capitalist conditions.

## Key quotes

- *"This story mirrors the trivial interplay between art's legitimating discourses… and an audience that, simply (and stupidly), asks what makes something art, asks what its 'punch line' is."* (line 41)
- *"The common denominator in this ugly synthesis is the demand for punch lines and *Mehrwert*."* (line 49)
- *"Kippenberger appropriated strips from [Akbar and Jeff] and altered them in minor ways, but he always left out the last image, so that the punch line never comes."* (line 53)
- *"this coalition defines artistic *Mehrwert* as that aspect of art which cannot be captured in words… this group sustains the justified call for a complex aesthetic experience that does not simply operate with key ideas and punch lines. But it also sustains a reactionary desire for total immersion."* (line 59)
- *"The artwork is not an image of the artist's singularity, nor is it an arbitrary sign. Rather, it continues to be regarded as an *index* of his or her uniqueness."* (lines 141–143)

## Why it's in the course

This is the course's diagnostic text for how contemporary art gets read, priced, and legitimated — especially the demand for a clean "punch line" that can be paraphrased in a wall label or an artist talk. Students working with AI/ML tools need a frame for resisting the curatorial reduction of their work to "AI did X."
