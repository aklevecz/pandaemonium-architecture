# Marc Andreessen (Andreessen Horowitz), *The Techno-Optimist Manifesto* (October 2023)

## Summary

Posted 16 October 2023 on a16z.com, Andreessen's manifesto is a litany of short credal paragraphs ("We believe…") proclaiming the moral rightness of unbounded technological acceleration, free markets, "Nick Land's techno-capital machine," and AI as a "universal problem solver." It names a closed canon of "patron saints" (from Ada Lovelace and Adam Smith through Nietzsche, John von Neumann, and Filippo Marinetti, plus e/acc Twitter pseudonyms @BasedBeffJezos and @bayeslord) and a matching "enemy" list — "existential risk," "sustainability," "ESG," "tech ethics," "trust and safety," the Precautionary Principle, and Nietzsche's "Last Man." The document reads as the explicit political theology of the VC class whose dollars fund the AGI race Gebru and Torres diagnose.

## Key quotes

- *"We believe any deceleration of AI will cost lives. Deaths that were preventable by the AI that was prevented from existing is a form of murder."* (line 241)
- *"Combine technology and markets and you get what Nick Land has termed the techno-capital machine, the engine of perpetual material creation, growth, and abundance."* (line 205)
- *"We believe Artificial Intelligence is our alchemy, our Philosopher's Stone – we are literally making sand think."* (line 235)
- *"We believe a Universal Basic Income would turn people into zoo animals to be farmed by the state. Man was not meant to be farmed; man was meant to be useful, to be productive, to be proud."* (line 187)
- *"Our present society has been subjected to a mass demoralization campaign for six decades — against technology and against life — under varying names like 'existential risk', 'sustainability', 'ESG', 'Sustainable Development Goals', 'social responsibility', 'stakeholder capitalism', 'Precautionary Principle', 'trust and safety', 'tech ethics', 'risk management', 'de-growth', 'the limits of growth'."* (line 453)
- *"Our enemy is Friedrich Nietzsche's Last Man … Our enemy is… that. We aspire to be… not that."* (lines 485–515)
