# Ben Grosser, *Techno-Optimist Manifesto* (Andreessen, redacted)

**Week:** 2 (Additional / Primary Documents)
**File:** `markdown/additional_reading_primary_documents/Techno-Optimist-Manifesto-Andreessen-redacted-by-Grosser.md`
**Length:** 322 lines

## Summary

An art-object rather than an argument: Grosser has taken Andreessen's October 2023 manifesto and redacted almost every sentence, leaving behind only the most incriminating residues — the verbs of growth, the imperatives of conquest, the lists of "enemies," and the Futurist-Nietzschean vocabulary the original text buries in VC boilerplate. What remains is a cadence of single words and fragments: *"grow or die"*, *"growth growth growth more growth more technology"*, *"violent assault"*, *"we are conquerors"*, *"We are the apex predator"*, *"aggression"*, *"We believe in the Silicon Valley code of grow."* The redaction turns a16z's credo into a concrete poem of accumulation. It is both critique and reading aid.

## Key quotes

- *"growth is technology. growth, growth, growth more growth : more growth, growth, growth, more growth more technology."* (line 37)

## Why it's in the course

Week 2 assigns the Andreessen manifesto *and* this redaction back-to-back. Grosser's piece is the course's canonical example of an artist operating on a primary political-technical document — a "ritual of reversal" (Vogt) applied to a16z prose. It sits alongside Bazzell's *Extreme Privacy* as two artistic/strategic responses to the same apparatus: redact, or disappear.

## Relevance to the lab

**Reversal / Zones of opacity (iv), with stance (i) in the loop.** This is the archetypal lab-stance-four object in the syllabus — the tool (in this case, the document itself) used against its native purpose of venture-capital evangelism. Students should read Grosser as a model project: a single-pass transformation whose art-coefficient (in Duchamp's sense) emerges precisely from what the redactor chose to leave visible. A strong W4–W6 lab prompt: "redact an apparatus you use daily; show us what's left."
