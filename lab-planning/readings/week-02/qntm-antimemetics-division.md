## Summary

Serialized novelette presented as SCP Foundation documents and narrative prose. Defines *antimemetics* as ideas, entities, or phenomena that resist being remembered, recorded, or spread. SCP-055 is a "self-keeping secret" — an object you can observe directly but cannot retain knowledge of; information about it "leaks" from minds. The protagonist Marion Wheeler heads a hidden Antimemetics Division tasked with containing memetic hazards that feed on cognition itself. The narrative explores how antimemetic entities consume information about themselves, operate through zones of opacity where observation fails, and how knowledge-suppression pills (mnestics) become necessary to maintain operational continuity.

## Why it's in the course

Week 2 pairs qntm with Asparouhova (non-fiction on antimemetics) and Benzel's *Thinking Machine Imaginaries*. The novel operationalizes antimemetics as an *aesthetic* — demonstrating how to design systems and artifacts that refuse legibility, make themselves unrememberable, and operate in the blind spots of observation.

## Relevance to the lab

Directly informs **Reversal / Zones of opacity**. Students can extract design principles from the narrative: what does it mean to create something that resists documentation? How do feedback loops break when the system itself is designed to corrupt the memory of interaction? The concept of "inverted containment" — a space where the anomaly cannot reach — becomes a model for zones of refusal within computational systems.
