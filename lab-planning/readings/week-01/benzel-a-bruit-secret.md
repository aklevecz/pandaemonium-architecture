## Summary

Benzel traces the genealogy of Duchamp's *Creative Act* through his 1949 conversation with Gregory Bateson, establishing feedback and interactivity as foundational cybernetic concepts underlying all subsequent art. The note weaves together three apparently separate threads — Duchamp's mediumistic theory of creation; Bateson's role in founding the CIA's "indirect methods of warfare"; and Duchamp's entanglement with organized crime through the Factor family's Stardust Las Vegas visit in 1963 — to argue that the artist–spectator feedback loop is itself a mechanism of covert influence. Duchamp's work operates not as intentional expression but as a channel through which meaning is *pulled into being* by the spectator. The Stardust trip becomes a historical pivot: 1963 is also the year of JFK's assassination and the last of six CIA–mob attempts on Castro.

- *"State Crimes Against Democracy are concerted actions or inactions by government insiders intended to manipulate democratic processes and undermine popular sovereignty."* (deHaven-Smith, line 471)

## Why it's in the course

Week 1 is the foundation week and this note establishes that Duchamp's *Creative Act* — the two-pole feedback loop between artist and spectator — is not merely an aesthetic principle but a cybernetic operation. Bateson, a CIA architect and Duchamp's interlocutor, embodied this feedback loop in real time; his work on feedback mechanisms in the 1942–1949 period fed directly into CIA doctrine on "indirect methods." The note argues that feedback is already a tool of control — a mechanism of invisibility and covert induction.

## Relevance to the lab

This note anchors **Creative Act** directly to cybernetic feedback and covert operations. It challenges students to recognize that "the spectator completes the work" is not a neutral aesthetic claim but describes how meaning (and control) is *induced* rather than *declared*. Also shadows **Apparatus**: Duchamp himself becomes a case study in how tools (art, galleries, casinos, intelligence agencies) arrive pre-loaded with intention and history.
