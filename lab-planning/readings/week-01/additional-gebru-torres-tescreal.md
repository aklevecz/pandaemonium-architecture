## Summary

Gebru and Torres coin the acronym "TESCREAL" — Transhumanism, Extropianism, Singularitarianism, (modern) Cosmism, Rationalism, Effective Altruism, and Longtermism — to name a family of overlapping Silicon Valley ideologies driving the race to build AGI. They trace this family directly to twentieth-century Anglo-American eugenics (via Julian Huxley, Nick Bostrom, Max More, Eliezer Yudkowsky, Ray Kurzweil, Ben Goertzel, et al.), argue that AGI cannot be engineered safely because it is definitionally unscoped, and show how billionaire funding (Open Philanthropy, Thiel, Musk, Altman) has legitimized the quest while its products continue to harm the same marginalized groups first-wave eugenics targeted. They close by urging researchers to abandon AGI and build well-scoped, task-specific systems instead.

## Key quotes

- *"We argue that, unlike systems with specific applications which can be evaluated following standard engineering principles, undefined systems like 'AGI' cannot be appropriately tested for safety."* (line 3)
- *"In this paper, we ask: What ideologies are driving the race to attempt to build AGI? To answer this question, we analyze primary sources by leading figures investing in, advocating for, and attempting to build AGI. Disturbingly, we trace this goal back to the Anglo-American eugenics movement, via transhumanism."* (line 24)
- *"The obsession with IQ can be traced back to first-wave eugenicists, who used IQ tests to identify 'defectives' and the 'feeble-minded.'"* (line 108)
- *"The AGI race is not an inevitable, unstoppable march towards technological progress, grounded in careful scientific and engineering principles. It is a movement created by adherents of the TESCREAL bundle seeking to 'safeguard humanity' by, in Altman's words, building a 'magic intelligence in the sky,' just like their first-wave eugenicist predecessors who thought they could 'perfect' the 'human-stock' through selective breeding."* (line 248)
- *"Attempting to build something akin to a god is an inherently unsafe practice, and [we] urge researchers and practitioners to abandon this goal in lieu of building well-defined, well-scoped systems that prioritize people's safety."* (line 256)

## Why it's in the course

Week 1's *TESCREAL, Libidinal Materialism, and AGI* segment uses this paper as the primary-source map of the ideological ecosystem the seminar keeps returning to: e/acc, longtermism, cosmists vs. terrans, the Thielverse, the Artilect War. Pairing Gebru/Torres with Selfridge (1958) and Vaswani (2017) gives students the full arc — the technical lineage *plus* the political lineage — of the systems they will meet in lab.

## Relevance to the lab

**Apparatus (i).** This is the clearest primary source for the "name what it decided before you arrived" move: when students open ChatGPT or an Anthropic model, Gebru and Torres name the eugenic utopianism already shaping the product roadmap, the safety-theater, and the benchmark choices. It also underwrites the lab's preference for narrow, well-scoped generative toys (Golly, Wotja, small local models) over engaging AGI-scale systems on their own hype terms.
