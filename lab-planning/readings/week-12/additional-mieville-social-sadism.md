## Summary

Miéville argues that contemporary capitalism has entered a new phase of unabashed, performative cruelty — *social sadism*. Traces the genealogy from Marie Antoinette's rustic fantasies to 2010 Halloween parties at a foreclosure law firm, where employees mocked the homeless while evicting them. Miéville refuses the comforting narrative of "banality of evil," insisting that cruelty is not aberration but functional to maintaining class rule. In neoliberalism's decadence, sadism becomes surplus: cruelty for cruelty's sake, entertainment in domination, a sign that the system has abandoned even the pretense of legitimacy.

## Key quotes

- *"This wasn't, then, some generalised, timeless jeer. It was more specific and pointed, gleeful malice at those whose lives were, at that very moment, being ruined, directed at them by those doing the ruining."* (line 45)
- *"Capitalism's history might be tracked in a genealogy of the corporate apology … Baum's quivering lip should provoke only piss and vinegar."* (line 52)
- *"Class rule necessitates violence and its contested, overlapping, jostling ideologies … rising unevenly from a fundamental necessity to capital — oppression — are brutalities necessary to sustain class rule at home; to sustain imperialism abroad; everyday sadisms so metabolised their cruelties often hide in plain sight."* (lines 69–79)

## Why it's in the course

Week 12 seminar explicitly lists Miéville under *Beclownment of Nightmares* alongside Fisher's SF Capital and the CCRU epigraph. Reframes cruelty as generative force, prefiguring algorithmic and financial violence. Pairs with Vogt's *Rituals of Reversal* (same week, additional) on the political-affective substrate of contemporary systems.

## Relevance to the lab

**Apparatus.** Names the sadism built into platforms (engagement via outrage, wealth extraction via labor precarity). A project that *reveals* this sadism risks neutralizing it through aesthetic framing (Miéville's own warning); a project that refuses the apology-genre and operates with "piss and vinegar" is a possible — deliberately uncomfortable — lab mode.
