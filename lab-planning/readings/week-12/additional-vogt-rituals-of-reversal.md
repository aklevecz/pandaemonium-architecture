## What I know about the text from the syllabus

Vogt's essay examines annual Highland Maya Zinacanteco rituals that
invoke the reversal of social, gender, and species roles as a way to
"rewire" the community's social structure. The essay is one of the two
course epigraphs on the homepage; the other is Dan McQuillan.

From the syllabus:

> *"…rituals of reversal…'rewire' the crucial connections in the social
> structure by providing symbolic statements of traditional social
> imperatives and basic categories of…world view."* — Vogt

And the syllabus gloss (line 11):

> *"Contemporary social structures are being 'rewired' with ever-
> increasing speed by often invisible algorithmic and predictive
> processes."*

So the course reads contemporary algorithmic systems as a *non-ritual*
form of the same kind of rewiring, and the course's counter-move — and
the lab's — is to enact *intentional, artistic* rituals of reversal
against them.

## Why it's in the course

Week 12 pairs Vogt with Mark Fisher's *SF Capital* and Nguyen's *Games,
Agency as Art* under the seminar heading *Rewiring Social Structure*.
The text is foundational enough that Scott puts it on the homepage as
epigraph — it is one of the two theses the entire course is organized
around.
